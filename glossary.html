Glossary of Terms

  • Code-switching - Alternating between two or more languages or dialects in a conversation
  • Creole Language - A pidgin language that has a generation of native speakers
  • Dialect - A form of a language that is specific to a particular region or social group
  • Language Policy - Laws, regulations, and rules that influence and/or plan the way language changes and develops within a group or region
  • Lexifier - The dominant language for a pidgin or creole language that determines most of its vocabulary
  • Loanword - A word taken from a different language with little or no modification
  • LWC - Language of Wider Communication, the language that people commonly use to communicate across language and cultural boundaries
  • Pidgin Language - A language that develops from the mixing and simplifying of other languages over a short period of time
